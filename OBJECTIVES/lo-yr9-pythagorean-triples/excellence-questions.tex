{\LARGE \textbf{Pythagorean triples — Excellence}}
\begin{tasks}(3)
\task Decide whether \mbox{$(20,21,29)$} is a Pythagorean triple.
\task Decide whether \mbox{$(11,60,61)$} is a Pythagorean triple.
\task Decide whether \mbox{$(13,84,85)$} is a Pythagorean triple.
\task Decide whether \mbox{$(16,30,34)$} is a Pythagorean triple in simplest form.
\task A student says \mbox{$(15,36,39)$} is not a Pythagorean triple because all three numbers have a common factor. Is the student correct?
\task Find the missing side: \mbox{$(20,\ ?,29)$}.
\task Find the missing side: \mbox{$(\ ?,36,39)$}.
\task Find the missing side: \mbox{$(12,\ ?,37)$}.
\task Find the missing side: \mbox{$(28,45,\ ?)$}.
\task Find a Pythagorean triple with hypotenuse \mbox{$65$}.
\task Find a Pythagorean triple with one short side \mbox{$24$} and simplest whole-number form.
\task A right-angled triangle has sides \mbox{$(6k,8k,10k)$}. If the hypotenuse is \mbox{$50$}, find \mbox{$k$} and the full triple.
\task A right-angled triangle has sides \mbox{$(5k,12k,13k)$}. If one short side is \mbox{$60$}, find the other two sides.
\task Two side lengths of a right-angled triangle are \mbox{$27$} and \mbox{$36$}. Show that the third side is a whole number.
\task Write a non-example: three whole numbers close to a Pythagorean triple that do not satisfy the theorem.
\task Explain the difference between a primitive Pythagorean triple and a scaled Pythagorean triple, using one example of each.
\end{tasks}
