{\LARGE \textbf{Using tables to explore patterns and graphs — Proficient}}\\[0.2em]
Use each table to find a rule, extend the pattern, or predict what the graph would look like.

\begin{tasks}(3)
\task {Find the rule and write the next value of $y$.\\[0.3em]
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
$x$ & $0$ & $1$ & $2$ & $3$ \\\hline
$y$ & $1$ & $4$ & $7$ & $10$ \\\hline
\end{tabular}}

\task {Find the rule and write the next value of $y$.\\[0.3em]
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
$x$ & $1$ & $2$ & $3$ & $4$ \\\hline
$y$ & $8$ & $6$ & $4$ & $2$ \\\hline
\end{tabular}}

\task {Find the rule and write the next value of $y$.\\[0.3em]
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
$x$ & $0$ & $2$ & $4$ & $6$ \\\hline
$y$ & $3$ & $7$ & $11$ & $15$ \\\hline
\end{tabular}}

\task {Complete the table for $y=4x-1$. Then write the ordered pair for $x=5$.\\[0.3em]
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
$x$ & $1$ & $2$ & $3$ & $4$ \\\hline
$y$ &  &  &  &  \\\hline
\end{tabular}}

\task {Complete the table for $y=12-3x$. Then decide whether the graph rises or falls.\\[0.3em]
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
$x$ & $0$ & $1$ & $2$ & $3$ \\\hline
$y$ &  &  &  &  \\\hline
\end{tabular}}

\task {Complete the table for $y=0.5x+1$. Then write the ordered pair for $x=8$.\\[0.3em]
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
$x$ & $0$ & $2$ & $4$ & $6$ \\\hline
$y$ &  &  &  &  \\\hline
\end{tabular}}

\task A table shows $(0,2)$, $(1,5)$, $(2,8)$, $(3,11)$. Write the rule and say whether the graph is a straight line or not.

\task A table shows $(1,9)$, $(2,7)$, $(3,5)$, $(4,3)$. Write the rule and predict the $x$-intercept.

\task A pattern has rule $y=2x+3$. Which is larger: the value when $x=7$ or the value when $x=10$? By how much?

\task A pattern has rule $y=15-x$. Write three ordered pairs and describe the slope in words.

\task The table is $(0,0)$, $(1,1)$, $(2,4)$, $(3,9)$. Is the graph likely to be a straight line or a curve? Explain from the table.

\task The table is $(0,5)$, $(2,9)$, $(4,13)$, $(6,17)$. Write a rule that matches the pattern and then write the point for $x=8$.
\end{tasks}
