Excellence

16 math tasks

Download tasks
  1. Draw two different valid nets for a cube. Explain how you know they are different.
  2. A student says, ``Any 6 joined squares make a cube net.'' Give a counterexample and explain it.
  3. A cuboid has dimensions $8 cm$ by $5 cm$ by $3 cm$. Draw a correct net and label all face dimensions.
  4. A student drew a cuboid net using only rectangles of size $8\times 5$ and $8\times 3$. What face type is missing, and why does that matter?
  5. Draw a net for a triangular prism with equilateral triangular ends of side $4 cm$ and prism length $9 cm$.
  6. Name all pairs of congruent faces in your triangular prism net.
  7. Draw a net for a square-based pyramid with base side $6 cm$ and triangular faces of slant edge $5 cm$.
  8. Explain why the 4 triangular faces of a square-based pyramid net are congruent when all side edges are equal.
  9. A cube net is made from $1 cm$ squares. What is the total area of the paper before folding?
  10. A cuboid net has two faces of area $24 cm^2$, two faces of area $18 cm^2$, and two faces of area $12 cm^2$. Find one possible set of cuboid dimensions.
  11. Explain why a net can show surface area directly but not volume directly.
  12. Design a net for a solid that is not a cube. State the solid, label its faces, and explain how you know the net is correct.
  13. A shape has 5 faces, 8 edges, and 5 vertices. Which basic solid could it be, and what might its net look like?
  14. Compare a cube net and a square-based pyramid net. Give 2 similarities and 2 differences.
  15. Why can two different nets fold to the same 3D shape? Use a cube or cuboid as your example.
  16. Explain how congruent faces help you check whether a cuboid net is correct.
